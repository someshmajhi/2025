About World Environment Day

World Environment Day is the biggest international day for the environment, celebrated annually on June 5th. Led by the United Nations Environment Programme (UNEP), it has become the largest global platform for environmental public outreach, celebrated by millions of people across the world. Its primary goal is to raise global awareness and action for the protection of our environment.

Theme: Beat Plastic Pollution

Why "Beat Plastic Pollution"?

Plastic pollution is a pervasive and urgent global issue. It pollutes our oceans, harms wildlife, and has even been found in our food and bodies as microplastics. The "Beat Plastic Pollution" campaign for World Environment Day 2025 calls for collective action to address this crisis.

The Republic of Korea, as the host country, has been a leader in tackling plastic waste with a comprehensive life-cycle plastic strategy, encouraging waste reduction at the source and promoting recycling and a circular economy.

How You Can Contribute

  • Reduce your plastic consumption: Opt for reusable bags, bottles, and containers.
  • Recycle properly: Understand your local recycling guidelines and dispose of plastic waste correctly.
  • Support sustainable businesses: Choose products with minimal or no plastic packaging.
  • Participate in clean-up drives: Join local initiatives to remove plastic from your community and natural spaces.
  • Educate others: Spread awareness about the impacts of plastic pollution and solutions.
  • Advocate for change: Encourage your local government and businesses to adopt stronger policies against plastic pollution.

Every action, no matter how small, contributes to a healthier planet.
